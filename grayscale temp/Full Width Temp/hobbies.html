Hobbies and Interests

A closer look at the various activities I'm passionate about.

Strava

I consistently record my activities on Strava, which is a social media app designed for runners and cyclists. I use it daily as a journal for my various activities. You can view and follow my profile here.

So far this year, I've logged roughly 147 hours of activities-- 600mi of running with the rest split between skiing and hiking. Strava also creates fun heatmaps, which show various places I've been with GPS data.





Skiing

I used to really dislike winter. Long, cold, and boring days seemed to drag on forever. Skiing completely changed this for me.

I was able to log over 50 days on the slopes this season at 7 different resorts. I don't actually enjoy the act of skiing as much as the scenery in the mountains, and the sport is a perfect excuse to take myself there.





Skiing: Price Breakdown

The sport has become extremely expensive for many, with day ticket prices a whopping $300 at many resorts here in Utah.

However, for those who consistently ski, the sport has never been more affordable. Many resorts have banded together and launched mega-pass products that allow access to several mountains. The following Tableau chart compares the prices and access of these various passes.



Running

The snow eventually melts, so I need some way to spend the warmer days.

I don't particularly enjoy running as much as skiing, cycling, or hiking, but there's no better way to maintain a high fitness level. "Runner's high" is also very real, and leads to some great feelings of accomplishment.



Cycling

Last year, after getting a stress fracture from running too much, I bought a road bike and discovered a new passion

It offered me a new and exciting way to explore the Utah mountains, and I even entered and completed a hundred-mile race at the end of the summer.
